# What Goes On In My Little Mind

Welcome to a corner of the internet where a medical student asks too many questions and is unfortunately allowed access to a keyboard.

This started with a simple frustration:human biology is usually taught as a collection of organs, diseases, pathways, and facts. Useful? Absolutely. Satisfying? errrrrm not quite.

I keep finding myself less interested in *what* the body does and more interested in *how* it maintains itself. Less interested in organs as structures and more interested in the visible invisible negotiations constantly taking place between them.

The heart isn't just pumping blood. The kidneys aren't just making urine. The brain isn't just thinking. Together, they're participating in a never-ending struggle to keep a highly chaotic organism alive, functional, and only mildly confused[well atleseagt as much as we can be].

So this isn't really a biology blog.

More like a written expression of my ever raging mind, of questions, observations, models, half-baked ideas, and occasional rabbit holes about how living systems work.

We'll explore:
Physiology as state maintenance rather than organ function
Disease as system drift, instability, or transition through state space
Intelligence, prediction, and biological control systems
The possibility that humans can be modeled as nested, interacting state systems across multiple scales

You'll find ideas that are established, ideas that are speculative,probably ragebait some big brains and ideas that will probably make future-me cringe.

That's fine.

The goal isn't to present final answers.

I aim to think out loud, follow interesting questions wherever they lead, and occasionally stumble into something useful.

If you're a clinician, researcher, engineer, student, founder, or just another curious human who enjoys asking "but why?" one too many times, you're in the right place, maybe.

Welcome to what goes on in my little mind.


_A note before you read: This series mixes established biology with my own attempts to build a more unified way of thinking about living systems. I'll always try to distinguish accepted science from my own interpretations as the framework evolves._
